DCA vs lump sum into BTC

Both strategies buy BTC at the spot close. Portfolio values evolve with BTC's daily price; the dotted line is DCA's running cost basis.

Verdict
On BTC, a lump-sum of $1,200 on day 1 is worth $990 today (-17.53%), while the same principal drip-fed monthly ended at $975 (-18.74%). The lump sum approach is ahead by $15. The gap between the solid DCA line and its dotted cost-basis shows the portfolio's running PnL; the gold line is what an all-in buyer has.

Fiat vs Bitcoin — which was more volatile?

Top: rolling 30-day annualised stdev of daily returns. Bottom: risk-return scatter — annualised volatility (Y) vs 1Y return (X).

Risk vs reward
Volatility is measured the same way as in the Q1 panel: the standard deviation of daily returns, annualised as stdev × √252 so it reads as a yearly percentage. BTC is the most volatile at 35.4% annualised, USD the calmest at 0.0%. BTC ranks #1 of 8 by daily-return stdev. The risk-return scatter plots this volatility (Y) against the 1Y total return from Q1 (X), so you can read each symbol's reward-per-unit-risk at a glance: points in the upper-left delivered loss with high volatility, lower-right means steady gains.
